Our Story

A History of Computing at SU

From the first mainframe to machine learning at scale — five decades of building a world-class department in the heart of the Cape Winelands.

Computing Comes to Stellenbosch

Stellenbosch University acquires its first mainframe computer, housed in the Faculty of Science. A small group of mathematicians and engineers begin teaching the foundations of programming and numerical computation to undergraduate students.

1969
1976

Department of Computer Science Established

Computer Science separates from the Department of Mathematics to become an independent academic department. The department's founding vision: to combine theoretical depth with the practical demands of computing in a rapidly modernising South Africa.

Postgraduate Programmes Launched

The department launches dedicated postgraduate programmes in Computer Science, attracting the first cohort of MSc and doctoral students. Research groups begin forming around key areas including formal methods, programming languages, and computer graphics.

1989
2001

Research Labs and Centres Established

Dedicated research laboratories are established, and the department joins national and international research initiatives. Focus areas expand to include computer networks, distributed systems, bioinformatics, and software engineering, reflecting the era's digital transformation.

National Centre of Excellence Recognition

The department's research programmes receive national recognition. Significant growth in postgraduate enrolment and externally funded research projects positions the department as one of South Africa's leading Computer Science research centres.

2012
2022

MSc in Machine Learning & AI Launched

The department launches the MSc in Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence — a structured, internationally-benchmarked programme preparing students for the frontier of AI research and industry. The programme draws students from across Africa and the world, cementing SU's position as a leading AI research hub on the continent.

Professor du Toit holds the Chair in Computing Systems and has led the department since 2018. His research spans formal verification, concurrent programming, and the mathematical foundations of computing. What Guides Us

Our Academic Pillars

Four principles that define how we work, teach, and pursue research.

science

Research Excellence

We pursue fundamental research questions at the highest international level. Our work is published in the world's leading venues, cited by peers globally, and driven by genuine intellectual curiosity rather than fashion.

school

Academic Rigour

We hold our students and ourselves to demanding standards of intellectual honesty, precision, and depth. We believe that mastering hard ideas — rather than skimming surfaces — is the foundation of all lasting capability.

lightbulb

Innovation and Impact

We translate discovery into solutions. Our research informs policy, seeds companies, and builds the infrastructure of South Africa's knowledge economy. Good ideas should not stay in papers — they should change the world.

diversity_3

Inclusive Excellence

We are building a department where talent thrives regardless of background. Diversity strengthens our science, broadens our perspective, and ensures that the technology we build serves all of society — not just the privileged few.
