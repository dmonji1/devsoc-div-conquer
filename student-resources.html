Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from students across all levels of study.

school Registration & Enrolment

How do I register for modules in a new semester? expand_more
Module registration is done through the SUNLearn portal. Log in with your student number, navigate to "Module Enrolment", and select your modules for the semester. Registration opens approximately three weeks before semester start. If a module is full or you have a prerequisite query, contact the departmental administrator.
Can I add or drop a module after registration closes? expand_more
Late additions require written motivation and departmental approval. Drops are allowed up to the published late-drop deadline without academic penalty. After this date, a module withdrawal is recorded on your transcript. Contact the undergraduate administrator at cs@sun.ac.za for assistance.
What happens if I fail to meet prerequisites? expand_more
You may not register for a module if you have not passed its listed prerequisites. In exceptional circumstances a lecturer may grant a prerequisite waiver in writing. This must be submitted to the administrative office before the registration deadline.

assignment Assessments & Examinations

How are module marks calculated? expand_more
Each module has a study guide specifying its mark components (practicals, tests, projects, and final exam) along with their respective weights. The study guide is distributed at the start of semester via SUNLearn. Typically, continuous assessment contributes 40–50% and the final examination 50–60%.
What is the minimum mark to pass a module? expand_more
A final module mark of 50% is required to pass. For modules with a compulsory final examination, you must also achieve at least 40% in the final exam to pass overall. Sub-minimum requirements are specified in each module's study guide.
How do I apply for a supplementary or aegrotat exam? expand_more
Supplementary exams are granted to students with final marks between 45–49%. Aegrotat (medical) applications require a medical certificate submitted to the Student Doctor within 48 hours of the missed exam, followed by a formal application via the faculty office. Both processes use the official SU Student Administration forms.

biotech Postgraduate & Research

How do I find a supervisor for my Master's or PhD? expand_more
Review the academic staff profiles and research areas to identify potential supervisors aligned with your interests. Contact potential supervisors directly by email with a brief research proposal and your academic transcript. The postgraduate coordinator can also assist in matching you with available supervisors.
What funding opportunities are available? expand_more
Funding sources include NRF bursaries, SU Postgraduate Scholarships, industry-funded research positions, and PGCHE teaching assistantships. Most supervisor-funded positions are advertised directly by the research group. Contact the postgraduate administrator for a current list of available bursaries.
How long does a PhD programme take? expand_more
The PhD in Computer Science is a research degree with a minimum duration of 2 years and a maximum of 5 years (for full-time students). Most students complete in 3–4 years. Part-time study is possible with approval. Progress is reviewed annually through a formal progression report submitted to the postgraduate committee.

computer IT & Systems Access

How do I get access to the department's computing labs? expand_more
Lab access is granted automatically upon registration for a Computer Science module. Your student card activates access to the labs in the CS building. If you experience access problems, contact the departmental IT officer or the administrative office in room A507.
Can I access departmental servers remotely? expand_more
Registered postgraduate students and final-year project students may request SSH access to departmental research servers. Submit a request via the departmental IT request form (available in Forms & Downloads below) with your supervisor's written approval.
What software licences are available to students? expand_more
Students have access to Microsoft 365 (via SU), JetBrains IDEs (student licence), MATLAB (campus licence), and various developer tools. GitHub Education benefits are also available through your @sun.ac.za student email. Visit the SU IT Help Desk portal for the full licence catalogue.

Key Procedures

  • arrow_right
    Mark queries must be submitted in writing to the module lecturer within 7 days of results being published. Include your student number and the specific concern.
  • arrow_right
    Remark requests are submitted via the Faculty of Science office and are subject to a remark fee (refundable if mark changes by 5% or more).
  • arrow_right
    Plagiarism declarations must accompany all written submissions. Violations are handled under the SU Student Disciplinary Code.
  • arrow_right
    Special concession requests (e.g. deadline extensions) require approval from both the lecturer and the programme coordinator. Submit with supporting documentation.
