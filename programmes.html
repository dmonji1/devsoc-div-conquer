Honours

BScHons Computer Science

assignment Admission Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree with Computer Science as major, or equivalent qualification
  • Average final mark of at least 60% for Computer Science 3
  • At least 32 credits at second- or third-year level in quantitative modules (Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Physics, Mathematical Statistics, or Operations Research)

calendar_today Applications & Duration

  • Apply online at student.sun.ac.za by 31 October of the preceding year
  • Late applications accepted until 30 November
  • Normal duration: 1 year; programme begins at the start of the academic year
  • Non-SU applicants: apply early as qualification verification takes additional time

Programme Structure — 128 credits total

The programme consists of 6 elective modules of 16 credits each, plus a compulsory Honours Project in Computer Science (32 credits). At most 2 modules may be taken from related departments with permission. Not all modules are offered every year.

Postgraduate

Masters Programmes

Two MSc routes — a research thesis or a structured taught programme with a research project.

Independent research on an approved topic under the supervision of an academic staff member, culminating in a thesis. The programme is entirely research-based with no coursework component. The department may require supplementary studies as determined by your supervisor on a case-by-case basis.

Admission Requirements

  • BScHons in Computer Science, or an approved equivalent qualification
  • Additional supplementary studies may be required at the discretion of your supervisor

Programme Content

CodeModuleCreditsSemester
828 Thesis: Computer Science 180 Both
After completing your research, you must submit a thesis to the satisfaction of the examiners and complete an oral examination.

A structured one-year programme for students with a strong mathematical and computational background. It equips you with machine learning and AI fundamentals as well as advanced techniques at the research forefront. The programme comprises three blocks of 60 credits each: compulsory core modules, elective modules, and a research project.

Admission Requirements

  • Honours degree in Applied Mathematics, Computer Science, Mathematics, or Mathematical Statistics
  • Or: Four-year BEng in Electrical Engineering
  • Or: Equivalent qualification in a field closely linked to machine learning
  • Demonstrable proficiency in Python or equivalent; comfortable with numerical linear algebra, multivariable calculus, and basic probability theory
  • Apply online by 31 October of the preceding year

Doctoral

Doctoral Programmes

The highest level of academic achievement — original research that makes a substantial contribution to knowledge in Computer Science.

A dissertation containing the results of your independent research is required. PhD candidates work at the absolute frontier of their chosen research area, under the close supervision of experienced academic staff. Admission requires an MSc in Computer Science or an approved equivalent qualification.

Programme Content

The highest academic qualification offered by the department. This degree requires published scientific works of high quality that contribute substantially to the enrichment of knowledge in Computer Science. Candidates must already hold a PhD and have an established record of significant scholarly contribution.
